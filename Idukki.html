IDUKKI

Idukki district, nestled in the heart of the Western Ghats in Kerala, is a picturesque haven that captures the essence of nature's beauty. Known for its captivating landscapes, lush greenery, and serene ambiance, Idukki unfolds a mesmerizing tapestry of hills, valleys, and water bodies.

Idukki is celebrated for its enchanting hill stations, with Munnar being a crown jewel. The vast expanses of tea plantations create a surreal, green carpet that stretches as far as the eye can see. The district is also home to the majestic Anamudi Peak, the highest point in South India, offering panoramic views of the Western Ghats.

BEST TIME TO VISIT IDUKKI

1.Winter in Idukki:Idukki transforms into a serene winter wonderland during the colder months. The temperature drops, and mist blankets the lush landscapes. Crisp air and clear skies make it an ideal time for nature walks and exploration. Residents and tourists alike cherish the cool ambiance, with occasional drizzles adding a touch of romance to the scenery. Warm layers become essential as the region experiences a gentle chill that beckons locals to cozy up around bonfires and savor the unique charm of Idukki in winter.

2.Summer in Idukki:As summer arrives in Idukki, the region undergoes a vibrant transformation. The sun bathes the surroundings in golden hues, and the temperature rises, inviting locals and visitors to indulge in refreshing escapades. The cool mountain breezes provide respite from the heat, making it an ideal season for outdoor activities. The hills are adorned with blooming flowers, creating a picturesque landscape. Summer in Idukki is characterized by clear skies, offering breathtaking views of the lush valleys and rolling hills, making it a perfect time to explore the natural beauty of the region.

3.Monsoon in Idukki:Idukki dons a different persona during the monsoon, becoming a lush and verdant paradise. Rain showers transform the landscape into a symphony of greens, with mist-covered mountains creating a mystical ambiance. The monsoon brings life to the flora and fauna, rejuvenating the entire region. While the rainfall can be intense, it adds a dramatic touch to the scenic beauty of Idukki. Exploring the misty landscapes and witnessing the cascading waterfalls during this season offers a unique and enchanting experience for those who appreciate the untamed beauty of nature.

MAIN TOURIST ATTRACTIONS

1.MUNNAR

Nestled in the Western Ghats, Munnar is a breathtaking hill station renowned for its expansive tea plantations, mist-covered hills, and cool climate. Visitors can explore the lush greenery of the tea estates, witness panoramic views from the Anamudi Peak, and enjoy the tranquility of places like Mattupetty Dam and Attukal Waterfalls

2.IDUKKI ARCH DAM

The Idukki Arch Dam, a marvel of engineering, stands tall across the Periyar River, forming the Idukki Reservoir. The panoramic views of the dam and the surrounding landscape are awe-inspiring. Tourists can visit the nearby Idukki Wildlife Sanctuary, adding a touch of nature to the architectural brilliance.

Entry time: Monday to Sunday - 10:00 am â€“ 5:00 pm

3.PERIYAR WILDLIFE SANCTUARY

Spanning across Idukki and Pathanamthitta districts, the Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary is a haven for wildlife enthusiasts. Boasting a rich biodiversity, the sanctuary is home to elephants, tigers, and a variety of bird species. A boat ride on Periyar Lake offers a unique opportunity to spot wildlife along the shores.

Entry time: Monday to Sunday - 6:00 am â€“ 7:00 pm

4.MATTUPETTY DAM

Surrounded by hills and tea plantations, Mattupetty Dam is a picturesque reservoir known for its tranquil waters. Visitors can take a boat ride on the dam and enjoy the stunning views of the surrounding landscape. The nearby Indo-Swiss Dairy Farm is another attraction worth exploring.

Entry time: Monday to Sunday - 9:30 am â€“ 5:00 pm

5.ANAMUDI PEAK

The Anamudi Peak, which stands tall in the Eravikulam National Park, is the pride of Kerala. It is the highest mountain peak in India outside the great Himalayan Mountain Ranges, loaded with rich flora and fauna. It is also a home to the largest surviving populations of Asian Elephants, endangered Gaurs, exotic Nilgiri Langurs, and lion tailed macaque. The Anamudi Peak is a paradise in the wild, which is covered with evergreen forests with some very beautiful rivers flowing inside the jungle. Tourists take a trek to the top of the mountain and enjoy the sight of beautiful rivers flowing into the east like Vaigai and Thamirabarani, and the Periyar River.

Entry time: Monday to Sunday - 8:30 am â€“ 4:00 pm

5.PAINAVU

Painavu, the headquarters of Idukki district, is a charming town surrounded by hills and forests. The town offers a peaceful retreat, and visitors can explore the nearby Kalvari Mount, a vantage point providing panoramic views of the Idukki Reservoir and surrounding landscapes.

Entry time: open all day
